## Board & Setup

### The Playing Field

- **Size:** 5 columns √ó 6 rows (smaller than standard 8√ó8 chess)
- **Starting Position:**
  - **White** pieces at the bottom (rows 5-6)
  - **Black** pieces at the top (rows 1-2)
  - **Light squares** (#E8D5B7) and **dark squares** (#0F172A)

### Piece Setup

Each player starts with:
- **1 King** (‚ôö) - Royal piece, most important
- **1 Queen** (‚ôõ) - Most powerful piece
- **1 Rooks** (‚ôú) - Strong, moves in straight lines
- **1 Knights** (‚ôû) - Jump in L-shapes
- **1 Bishop** (‚ôù) - Moves diagonally
- **5 Pawns** (‚ôü) - Weakest, move forward one square

## Piece Movement

### ‚ôî King
- Moves **1 square** in any direction (up, down, left, right, diagonal)
- **Most important piece** - if your king is captured, you lose
- Cannot move into danger or adjacent to opponent's king

### ‚ôï Queen
- Moves **any number of squares** in any direction (horizontal, vertical, diagonal)
- **Most powerful piece** - can reach almost any square
- Cannot jump over other pieces

### ‚ôñ Rook
- Moves **any number of squares** horizontally or vertically
- Powerful for controlling rows and columns
- Cannot jump over other pieces

### ‚ôó Bishop
- Moves **any number of squares** diagonally
- Each bishop controls squares of only one color (light or dark)
- Cannot jump over other pieces

### ‚ôò Knight
- Moves in an **L-shape:** 2 squares in one direction + 1 square perpendicular
- **Only piece that can jump** over other pieces
- Example: From middle square, can jump to 8 possible L-shaped positions

### ‚ôô Pawn
- Moves **1 square forward** (toward opponent)
- **Captures diagonally** forward (1 square)
- First move can be 2 squares forward (if no piece blocks)
- Cannot move backward
- **Special:** If a pawn reaches the opposite end, it **promotes** to Queen, Rook, Bishop, or Knight!

## Game Rules

### Taking Turns

1. **White moves first** (always)
2. Players alternate: White ‚Üí Black ‚Üí White ‚Üí ...
3. You must move one piece per turn

### Capturing Pieces

- Move your piece to a square with an opponent's piece to **capture** it
- The opponent's piece is removed from the board
- **Cannot capture your own pieces**

### Check ‚ö†Ô∏è

- Your king is **in check** when an opponent's piece threatens to capture it
- **You must escape check immediately:**
  - Move your king away
  - Block with another piece
  - Capture the attacking piece
- Illegal moves (that leave king in check) are not allowed

### Legal Moves

- Highlighted **green squares** show where your selected piece can legally move
- **Light green:** Peaceful move (no capture)
- **Red highlighted:** Squares where you can capture opponent's pieces

## Special Moves

### ‚ôô Pawn Promotion

When a **pawn reaches the far end** of the board:
- A dialog appears asking which piece to promote to
- **Choose wisely:** Usually upgrade to a **Queen** (most powerful)
- Other options: Rook, Bishop, Knight
- The pawn is removed and replaced with your choice

### Captured Pieces Display

- **Left panel:** Black pieces captured by White
- **Right panel:** White pieces captured by Black
- Helps you track material advantage

## Winning & Losing

### ‚úÖ Checkmate (You Win!)

- Opponent's king is in **check** AND has **no legal moves**
- Game ends immediately
- The app displays: **"[Your Color] wins by checkmate"**

### ü§ù Stalemate (Draw)

- Opponent is **NOT in check** but has **no legal moves**
- Game ends as a draw
- The app displays: **"Draw by stalemate"**

### Piece Values (For Strategy)

- Pawn = 1 point
- Knight = 3 points
- Bishop = 3 points
- Rook = 5 points
- Queen = 9 points
- King = Priceless! (Don't lose it)

## Tips for Beginners

### Opening (First Few Moves)

1. **Control the center** - Occupy middle squares for flexibility
2. **Develop pieces** - Get knights and bishops into play
3. **Protect your king** - Don't leave it exposed early
4. **Don't rush** - Think ahead before moving

### Middle Game Strategy

1. **Look before you move** - Check all opponent's pieces can't capture yours
2. **Trade fairly** - Don't give away valuable pieces for cheaper ones
3. **Create threats** - Attack opponent pieces and squares simultaneously
4. **Protect your pieces** - Never leave pieces undefended

### Endgame (Few Pieces Left)

1. **King becomes active** - Use it to attack and defend
2. **Pawn promotion is key** - Push pawns toward victory
3. **Rooks and Queens dominate** - Use them to control board
4. **Watch for checkmate patterns** - Force opponent into corners

### Common Beginner Mistakes ‚ùå

- ‚ùå Moving pieces without checking if opponent can capture
- ‚ùå Ignoring opponent's threats
- ‚ùå Losing your queen early for no reason
- ‚ùå Leaving king unprotected in the center
- ‚ùå Promoting pawn to anything but Queen (usually)

### How to Get Better ‚úÖ

- ‚úÖ Play multiple games against AI
- ‚úÖ Think 2-3 moves ahead
- ‚úÖ Learn basic opening moves
- ‚úÖ Practice recognizing checkmate patterns
- ‚úÖ Try harder difficulty levels

## Troubleshooting

### "I can't move my piece!"

**Possible reasons:**
1. It's not your turn (timer shows opponent's turn)
2. Move would leave your king in check (illegal)
3. No legal moves available from that position
4. Game is over (checkmate/stalemate)

### "Where's the checkmate option?"

- **Automatic!** When checkmate happens, game ends and displays winner
- You can't move after checkmate - click "Back to Menu" to start new game

### "Why is AI so fast?"

- AI doesn't need thinking time for easy/medium
- Hard mode takes 1-2 seconds (normal, it's calculating)
- This is intentional - gives you time to see moves
